Life After Day Zero

Life After Day Zero is a weekly column that gives you useful information for surviving the Zombie Apocalypse. In reality we will be covering a wide range of topics that relate to getting by in any real world survival situation. Each weekly article will be either a stand alone piece or an installment of the Day Zero series which will be an ongoing time line that will have each article build upon the prior, starting with day Zero (the day after the apocalypse).

Day Zero; the last day of the world created by man and the first day of the world survived by him. In this publication, I have compiled a series of articles I've written on Self Education, Preparation and more importantly Survival. I use Zombies as metaphors to make things interesting and fun. I will touch on key factors of prepping for any and all potential disasters and the survival strategies and tactics used to survive them. I will present to you methods and strategies that encompass everything you will need for day to day living in a world where you will no longer be able to just walk down to the corner store and purchase what you need. I would; however, like to first make one statement. Everything about prepping and survival is completely, utterly, and totally situational, bases specifically on the scenario as it is presented to you. This is a concept that I see sorely misunderstood by most wood be preppers out there. With that having been said let us begin…

Survival is 100% all about mitigation; so, keep that one word; that one idea in the front of your mind. ALWAYS...

DEFINITIONS:

TZA -

The Zombie Apocalypse

SM -

Survival Mode, "The tactics and/or strategies used to survive a given situation".

CAPD -

Clear and Present Danger, "The dangers posed by a situation you might find yourself facing from any and all sources".

Prepping -

Steps taken to increase your chances of survival in any survival situation.

BN -

Base Needs, "Your basic or minimum needs during a survival situation".

IN -

Improved Needs, "The level to which you are able to improve your ability to provide for your BN".

ISR -

Initial Survival Rate, "This term refers to the number or magnitude of displaced survivors during a survival situation".

Scope -

The degree to which a disaster; manmade or otherwise, effectively disrupts the normal day to day operation of your community. Scope does not refer, just to how widespread the disruption is, but to the length of time that the disruption persists.

Weight -

The level of importance something is given.

Bugout -

The decision to leave the immediate area for a safer position.

Hunker Down -

The decision to stay put and try to ride out the situation.

Darwinism -

The theory or more to fact the law that states only the strongest or smartest shall survive.

In the interests of keeping all the information I have to offer in this ongoing column easy to understand I have ordered everything into three basic areas. They are ordered by Weight.
